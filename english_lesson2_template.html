üìö Review - Key Terms

Rhetoric

Persuasive device used to convince an audience

Logos

Logical appeals that use reason and facts

Pathos

Emotional appeals that target feelings

Ethos

Ethical appeals based on values and credibility

Language-based Rhetoric

Literary devices like metaphors and questions

üéØ Classification Practice

Classify these rhetorical devices as: Logos, Pathos, Ethos, or Language-based
1. "Would you let your child drive a car without a licence?"
Correct Answer: Language-based (rhetorical question)
2. "Teachers will get frustrated with the licence if students cannot use them correctly."
Correct Answer: Pathos (appeals to teachers' emotions)
3. "It would be unpatriotic not to introduce a licence for mobile phones for year 7."
Correct Answer: Ethos (appeals to patriotic values)
4. "If students have access to mobile phones, then they will become addicted to them."
Correct Answer: Logos (logical cause and effect)

üß† Logos - Logical Rhetoric

Logos:

  • Uses logic to persuade
  • Appeals to an audience's intelligence
  • Shows respect for an audience
  • Uses facts about a situation
  • Regarded as more persuasive than other forms of rhetoric
Remember DCCERS:
Definitional | Cause and effect | Comparison
Evidence and examples | Rebuttal | Speculative

‚ö° Logos - Cause and Effect

What is Cause and Effect?

Statements about how specific actions lead to desirable or undesirable results.

Examples:
"If vegetarianism continues to grow in popularity, then the meat industry will suffer."

"The growth in popularity of vegetarianism means the meat industry will suffer."
Practice: Convert to "If...then" format
Original: "Using mobile phones effectively improves student grades."
Write your answer in "If...then" format:
Sample Answer: "If students learn to use their mobile phones effectively, then their grades will improve."
Original: "Having additional bins encourages people to dispose of rubbish more thoughtfully."
Write your answer in "If...then" format:
Sample Answer: "If people have access to additional bins, then they are likely to dispose of their rubbish more thoughtfully."

üìä Logos - Evidence and Examples

What is Evidence and Examples?

Using examples, especially statistics, case studies and research. This material is effective when used to support other logics.

Example:
"Rates of extinction are increasing due to land clearing. Between 200 and 2000 extinctions occur every year."
Mobile Phone Example:
"In schools that have introduced mobile phone licences, the rate of online bullying has dropped by 50%."

üîç Classification Challenge

Classify as 'Cause and Effect' or 'Evidence and Examples':
1. "If we continue to drive petrol cars, pollution in cities will only increase."
Correct Answer: Cause and Effect
2. "The use of solar panels in Australia reduced carbon dioxide emissions by trillions of tonnes in 2020."
Correct Answer: Evidence and Examples
3. "Solar panels on private homes have made little impact on greenhouse gas emissions because other forms of pollution have increased."
Correct Answer: Evidence and Examples
4. "Without increasing the number of electric vehicles in Australia, pollution will continue to rise."
Correct Answer: Cause and Effect
