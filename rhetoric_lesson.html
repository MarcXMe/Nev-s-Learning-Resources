Examples:

üß† Logos - Logic and Reason

Definition:

Logos = Rhetoric that uses logic to persuade, commonly using cause and effect statements with evidence and examples.

Examples:

"If we all get our mobile licence, then the teacher will allow us to use phones in class."
"If we are required to have a mobile phone licence, then parents will not be able to keep their children safe."

Definition:

Pathos = Rhetoric that appeals to people's emotions and feelings (happy, sad, fearful, excited, etc.)

Examples:

"Parents are terrified about what their children might be doing with mobile phones."

ü§ù Ethos - Values and Beliefs

Definition:

Ethos = Rhetoric that connects with the audience through shared beliefs or values (helps people feel they belong)

Examples:

"Traditionally, milestones like driving a car have been marked with licences."
"It would be un-Australian to require a licence to use a mobile."

üìù Language-Based Rhetoric

Definition:
