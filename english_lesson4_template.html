🏛️ Ethos - Appeals to 'Character'

Ethos:

  • Appeals to audience's belief that a person, place, organisation or nation can have a character
  • Effective for engaging audiences with similar beliefs and values
  • Audiences could misinterpret message
  • Generates a 'gut feeling' response (does not rely on evidence)
Remember PPHAV:
Patriotism and nationalism | Progress | History and tradition
Authority | Values

🇦🇺 Ethos - Patriotism and Nationalism

Patriotism and Nationalism

Appeals to the audience's national identity. Can be used to divide people when ideas and people are labelled.

Example: "As Australians we must stick together, and support locally made products."
Practice: Identify patriotic/nationalistic words
1. "Protecting our natural resources is the most patriotic thing we can do."
Identify the patriotic words:
Sample Answer: "our" and "patriotic" - appeals to national identity and duty
2. "Recycling is your patriotic duty."
Identify the patriotic words:
Sample Answer: "patriotic duty" - appeals to national responsibility
3. "It would be un-Australian to throw your rubbish on the street."
Identify the patriotic words:
Sample Answer: "un-Australian" - implies going against national values
4. "If you really love your home, there is no way that you would rubbish it."
Identify the patriotic words:
Sample Answer: "love your home" - appeals to emotional connection with place/country

🚀 Ethos - Progress

Progress

Use words that suggest the future will be better – without necessarily using evidence and examples.

Example: "If we embrace renewable energy, Australia's future will be cleaner and greener."
Compare Ethos vs Logos:
Ethos (Progress):
"Recycling more plastic will mean our parks are cleaner and better for our animals."
Logos (Evidence):
"Increasing our plastic recycling from 9% to 20% will remove 5 tonnes of plastic from our parks each year."

🔍 Classification Challenge

Identify which statements use logos and which use progress ethos:
1. "Increasing the number of plastic bags that we recycle protects native animals."
Correct Answer: Progress Ethos (suggests better future without specific evidence)
2. "A 10% reduction in greenhouse gases will limit global warming to less than 1 degree Celsius."
Correct Answer: Logos (uses specific statistics and evidence)
3. "Using your car less will keep the planet cool."
Correct Answer: Progress Ethos (suggests better future without specific data)
4. "Switching your appliances off at the power point can reduce your carbon emissions by more than 12%."
Correct Answer: Logos (provides specific percentage and measurable outcome)
