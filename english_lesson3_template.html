üíù Pathos - Emotive Rhetoric

Pathos:

  • Appeals to an audience's emotions
  • Powerful emotive rhetoric connects with an audience
  • Humans have evolved to respond to emotions
  • Emotional rhetoric can backfire!
Remember SHAPPEIT:
Self interest | Humour | Adversarial attack | Personal attack
Psychological appeals | Emotive words | Inclusive rhetoric | Testimonial

üé≠ Pathos - Emotive Words

Emotive Words: Words that invoke deep feelings (for example, fear, joy, hatred, love, sadness).
Compare these examples:
Negative Example:
"Poor, defenceless animals are mistreated every day to test beauty products. There is nothing beautiful about their miserable tears as they cry unheard."
Positive Example:
"Stunning dogs were enhanced with beauty products. The owner's joyful tears were beautiful to watch as they reunited with their pets."

‚úèÔ∏è Emotive Language Practice

Alter the tone by changing emotive language:
Original: "Alice was excited about how much money she received when she recycled her bottles and cans."
Rewrite with different emotion:
Sample Answer: "Alice was disappointed about how much money she received when she recycled her bottles and cans."
Original: "Naresh was upset when he realised how many plastic bags he could recycle."
Rewrite with different emotion:
Sample Answer: "Naresh was optimistic when he realised how many plastic bags he could recycle."

ü§≤ Pathos - Self Interest

Self Interest: Appeals to the audience's desire to look after themselves first. This is most effective when logical rhetoric is reworded.
Converting Logos to Self Interest:
Cause & Effect (Logos): "Students who walk to school become fitter and healthier."
Self Interest (Pathos): "If you walk to school more often, you will feel much fitter and healthier."
Practice: Convert these to self interest statements
Original: "If vegetarianism continues to grow in popularity, then the meat industry will suffer."
Convert to self interest:
Sample Answer: "Becoming a vegetarian will improve your health and mean less days feeling sick."
Original: "If students learn to use their mobile phones effectively, then their grades will improve."
Convert to self interest:
Sample Answer: "Learning how to use your mobile phone more effectively will improve your grades."

üîç Classification Challenge

Classify as 'Self Interest' or 'Emotive Language':
1. "People who recycle their plastic waste save hundreds of dollars each year."
Correct Answer: Self Interest (focuses on personal financial benefit)
2. "Poor, defenceless native animals will continue to suffer terrible deaths if we do not recycle more plastic."
Correct Answer: Emotive Language (uses words like "poor," "defenceless," "suffer," "terrible")
3. "It was with great pride that the class weighed all the plastic waste they had collected on Clean Up Australia Day."
Correct Answer: Emotive Language (uses "great pride" to evoke positive emotions)
4. "You will see a lot more native animals in your local park if you recycle all your plastic waste."
Correct Answer: Self Interest (focuses on "you" and personal benefit of seeing more animals)
